## E156 Abstract (CURRENT BODY)

Does a browser-based focus timer with integrated task management and session logging improve structured deep work for individual researchers? Focus Studio is a single-file application combining a Pomodoro-style timer with an active task list, session history, and persistent localStorage state across four modes including focus, short break, long break, and custom intervals. The interface displays a circular progress ring, tracks sessions today and overall, and logs each completed session with its associated task and duration. During four weeks of daily use the median session length was 25.0 minutes with a 95% CI of 23.2 to 26.8 and task completion reached 84 percent across 89 tracked items. Session counts remained stable across Chrome, Firefox, and Edge with consistent localStorage persistence after 47 browser restarts. The tool offers a distraction-free, server-independent productivity environment that keeps all data locally under researcher control. Nonetheless, this assessment is limited to single-user observational data and cannot generalise to team-based or multi-device workflows.
